This paper investigates domain adaptation theory as a defense mechanism against adversarial reinforcement learning agents in penetration-testing simulations. By introducing controlled network changes that exploit domain shift, we demonstrate that malicious DQN agents can be deterred from effectively learning attack strategies — giving the blue team a principled way to impose a moving-target environment.

Presented at the IEEE Systems and Information Engineering Design Symposium (SIEDS) 2023 at UVA and at HammerCon 2023.
